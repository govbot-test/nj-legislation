SYNOPSIS

     "Restore Local Offshore Wind Control Act"; prohibits construction of certain offshore wind project infrastructure without local governmental approval.

 

CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT

     Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel.

  


An Act concerning local governmental approvals related to offshore wind projects, and amending and supplementing P.L.2010, c.57.

 

     Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

 

     1.  (New section) Sections 1 through 4 of P.L.    , c.    (C.          ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) shall be known and may be cited as the "Restore Local Offshore Wind Control Act."

 

     2.  (New section) The Legislature finds and declares that:

     a.  New Jersey has a long history of empowering local governments to regulate matters which greatly impact local residents.

     b.  The construction of offshore wind energy projects and related infrastructure will have major effects on nearby residents, including soil disturbance, air and water pollution, noise, and aesthetic depreciation, along with the associated decrease in property values; and

     c.  It is fitting that local governments, which are most in touch with the wishes of their constituents, should have the right to approve or disapprove of the construction of offshore wind related infrastructure within their jurisdictions.

 

     3.  (New section) As used in this act:

     "Board" means the Board of Public Utilities.

     "Local government unit" means a county, a municipality, or any board, commission, committee, authority, or agency thereof that is subject to the provisions of the "Local Public Contracts Law," P.L.1971, c.198 (C.40A:11-1 et seq.), including a housing authority or redevelopment agency created or continued under the "Local Redevelopment and Housing Law," P.L.1992, c.79 (C.40A:12A-1 et seq.).

     "Open access offshore wind transmission facility" means the same as the term is defined in section 3 of P.L.1999, c.23 (C.48:3-51).

     "Qualified offshore wind project" means the same as the term is defined in section 3 of P.L.1999, c.23 (C.48:3-51).

 

     4.  (New section) a.  The developer of a qualified offshore wind project or open access offshore wind transmission facility shall not place, replace, construct, reconstruct, install, reinstall, add to, extend, use, operate, inspect, and maintain wires, conduits, lines, and associated infrastructure, whether within, under, or upon the public streets, thoroughfares, or rights-of-way owned by a local government unit, unless the governing body of the local government unit has issued a written approval of the action.

     b.  The developer of qualified offshore wind project or open access offshore wind transmission facility may request to obtain easements, rights-of-way, or other real property interests on, over, or through any real property owned by a local government unit that are reasonably necessary for the construction or operation of the qualified offshore wind project or open access offshore wind transmission facility.  The developer shall first file a petition with the board seeking authority to obtain the easement, right-of-way, or other real property interest.  In considering a petition submitted pursuant to this subsection, the board shall conduct, or cause to be conducted, a public hearing in order to provide an opportunity for public input on the petition.  Notice of the public hearing shall be given in a manner and form as determined by the board in order to provide an opportunity for public input to be received on the petition.  At a minimum, notice of the public hearing shall be provided to the news media, the owner of the real property subject to the petition, the governing body and municipal clerk of the municipality, and the clerk of the county in which the lands right-of-way, or other real property interest.  The proposal shall not proceed without the local government unit's approval.

     c.  A developer who seeks to install or maintain open access offshore wind transmission facility infrastructure or qualified offshore wind project infrastructure pursuant to subsection a. of this section or to obtain property rights pursuant to subsection b. of this section, shall pay the full costs of those actions, including reimbursement to the board and local government unit for the reasonable costs of reviewing the proposed action.

STATEMENT

 

     This bill, to be known as the "Restore Local Offshore Wind Control Act," would prohibit the construction of certain offshore wind project infrastructure without local governmental approval.

     Specifically, the bill provides that a developer of a "qualified offshore wind project" or "open access offshore wind transmission facility," as those terms are defined in current law, may not place, replace, construct, reconstruct, install, reinstall, add to, extend, use, operate, inspect, and maintain wires, conduits, lines, and associated infrastructure, whether within, under, or upon the public streets, thoroughfares, or rights-of-way of any local government unit, unless the governing body of the local government unit has issued a written approval of the action.  In addition, the bill would require the developer to petition the Board of Public Utilities (BPU) when seeking to obtain an easement, right-of-way, or other real property interest on, over, or through any real property owned by a local government unit for the purposes of the offshore wind project.  The BPU would be required to hold a public hearing on the petition, review it, and provide a recommendation to the applicable local government unit regarding whether the real property interest is reasonably necessary for the offshore wind project.  The local government unit would then have 90 days to approve or disapprove of the developer's proposal to obtain the real property interest.  The bill would also require the developer to cover the costs of (1) the installation and maintenance of offshore wind project infrastructure, and (2) the review process for obtaining real property interests from a local government unit.

     The bill would also repeal portions of P.L.2021, c.178, which amended P.L.2010, c.57 (C.48:3-87.1) to grant various statutory authorities to qualified offshore wind projects and open access offshore wind transmission facilities and the BPU regarding the construction of offshore wind projects.  P.L.2021, c.178 authorized qualified offshore wind projects and open access offshore wind transmission facilities approved by the BPU to obtain easements, rights-of-way, or other real property interests on, over, or through any real property owned by a municipality, county, or other instrumentality of the State that are necessary for the construction of a qualified offshore wind project.  P.L.2021, c.178 also authorized the BPU to issue an order approving these kinds of real property interest acquisitions, upon petition by a qualified offshore wind project or an open access offshore wind transmission facility, if the BPU determined that the acquisition was necessary for the construction of an offshore wind project.  This bill would revoke these grants of authority, as well as the other, related, provisions contained in the same subsection.
