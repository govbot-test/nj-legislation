SYNOPSIS

     Consolidates certain transportation entities and transfers powers and duties of such entities to DOT.

 

CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT

     Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel.

  


An Act consolidating the States transportation entities within the New Jersey Department of Transportation, supplementing Title 27 of the Revised Statutes, and revising various parts of the statutory law.

 

     Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

 

     1.    (New Section)  The Legislature finds and declares that the States transportation system, as one of the most extensive in the United States, is of vital importance to its residents and the nation.  Due to its size and significance, New Jerseys transportation system requires a greater level of government oversight to ensure that all persons and industries served by the States transportation system receive high-quality and safe transportation service.  By consolidating several of the States major transportation entities within the Department of Transportation, New Jersey can improve its transportation planning and facilitate more efficient operations.  Moreover, it allows the State to make critical infrastructure improvements to meet the needs of those individuals and entities dependent on our States transportation system with greater speed and flexibility.

 

     2.    (New section) a.  As used in this section: 

     Department means the New Jersey Department of Transportation.

     Transfer Date means the date on which the department has certified to the Governor and each presiding officer of the Legislature, pursuant to subsection d. of this section, that the department has assumed the powers, duties, assets, and responsibilities of each transportation entity.

     Transportation entity means the New Jersey Turnpike Authority established pursuant to section 3 of P.L.1948, c.454 (C.27:23-3); the South Jersey Transportation Authority established pursuant to section 4 of P.L.1991, c.252 (C.27:25A-4); or the New Jersey Transit Corporation established pursuant to P.L.1979, c.150 (C.27:25-1 et seq.).

     b.    Until the Transfer Date, the Department of Transportation shall not exercise any powers, rights, or duties conferred by P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), or by any other law, in any way that interferes with the powers, rights, and duties of the New Jersey Turnpike Authority, the South Jersey Transportation Authority, or the New Jersey Transit Corporation.  Before the Transfer Date, the department shall not exercise any powers of the transportation entities.  The department and the transportation entities shall cooperate with each other so that the Transfer Date shall occur as soon as practicable after the date of enactment of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).  The department and the transportation entities shall make available information concerning their property and assets, outstanding bonds and other debts, obligations, liabilities, contracts, operations, and finances as the department may require to retire any outstanding bonds, notes, or other obligations of the transportation entities and efficiently exercise all powers, rights, and duties conferred upon the department by P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).

     c.     On the Transfer Date: 

     (1)   each transportation entity shall be consolidated within the department, and the department shall assume all powers, rights, assets, and duties of the transportation entities, to the extent provided by P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), and such powers shall then and thereafter be vested in and exercised by the department;

     (2)   the terms of office of the members of each transportation entity shall terminate, the officers having custody of the funds of a transportation entity shall deliver those funds into the custody of the chief financial officer of the department, the property and assets of each transportation entity shall, without further act or deed, become the property and assets of the department, and each transportation entity shall be consolidated within the department;

     (3)   the officers and employees of each transportation entity shall be transferred to the department and become employees of the department until determined otherwise by the department.  Nothing in P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) shall be construed to deprive any officers or employees of a transportation entity of their rights, privileges, obligations, or status with respect to any pension or retirement system.  The employees shall retain all their rights and benefits under existing collective negotiation agreements or contracts until new or revised agreements or contracts are agreed to.  All existing employee representatives shall be retained to act on behalf of those employees until the employees shall, pursuant to law, elect to change those representatives.  Nothing in P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) shall affect the civil service status, if any, of those officers or employees;

     (4)   all debts, liabilities, and obligations incurred, and all contracts entered into, by a transportation entity prior to the Transfer Date, except to the extent specifically provided or established to the contrary in P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), are imposed upon the department, and all creditors of a transportation entity and persons having claims against or contracts with a transportation entity of any kind or character may enforce those debts, claims, and contracts against the department as successor to a transportation entity in the same manner as they might have had against a transportation entity, and the rights and remedies of those holders, creditors, and persons having claims against or contracts with a transportation entity shall not be limited or restricted in any manner by P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill);

     (5)   in continuing the functions, contracts, obligations, and duties of each transportation entity, the department may act in its own name, or in the name of a transportation entity, as may be convenient or advisable under the circumstances from time to time;

     (6)   the assets of a transportation entity shall not be used by the department for non-transportation related purposes;

     (7)   the department shall not be authorized to issue new bonds, notes, or other obligations in its own name or in the name of any transportation entity under the powers transferred from each transportation entity to the department pursuant to this section;

     (8)   notwithstanding any law, rule, or regulation to the contrary, a transportation entity shall no longer be authorized to issue new bonds, notes, or other obligations;

     (9)   any references to the New Jersey Turnpike Authority, the South Jersey Transportation Authority, or the New Jersey Transit Corporation in any other law, rule, or regulation shall be deemed to refer and apply to the department;

     (10)  the rules and regulations of each transportation entity shall continue in effect as the rules and regulations of the department until amended, supplemented, or rescinded by the department in accordance with law.  Notwithstanding the requirements of the Administrative Procedure Act, P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et seq.) to the contrary, the department may adopt rules and regulations, after notice and an opportunity for public comment, amending, supplementing, modifying, or repealing the regulations of the department or any transportation entity.  Such rules and regulations shall be effective immediately upon filing with the Office of Administrative Law and shall be effective for a period not to exceed 18 months from the Transfer Date and may thereafter be amended, adopted, or readopted in accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act, P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et seq.).  Rules and regulations of any transportation entity that are inconsistent with the provisions of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), or with the provisions of rules and regulations of the department, shall be deemed void if so judged by the department acting pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph;

     (11)  the operations of each transportation entity shall continue as operations of the department until altered by the department as may be permitted pursuant to P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill); and

     (12)  the powers vested in the department by P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) shall be construed as being in addition to and not in diminution of the powers vested by law in each transportation entity prior to the effective date of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) to the extent not otherwise altered or provided for in P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).

     d.    As soon as practicable after the Transfer Date, the department shall notify the Governor and the presiding officer of each house of the Legislature: 

     (1)   that the transfer has occurred;

     (2)   of the date of the transfer; and

     (3)   of any other information concerning the transfer that the department deems appropriate.

     e.     On and after the Transfer Date, no officer or employee of the department shall be granted permanent tenure at the department.

STATEMENT

 

     This bill consolidates the New Jersey Turnpike Authority, the South Jersey Transportation Authority, and the New Jersey Transit Corporation (collectively, transportation entities) within the New Jersey Department of Transportation (department) and transfers the powers and duties of the transportation entities to the department.  The bill also amends current law to clarify this transfer of powers and duties.

     Specifically, on the Transfer Date specified in the bill, the outstanding bonds of each transportation entity are to be retired, the department is to assume all assets, debts, liabilities, and statutory responsibilities of the transportation entities, and each transportation entity is to be consolidated within the department.  In addition, the employees of each transportation entity are to be transferred to the department and become employees of the department; any union contracts for the transportation entities are to continue in effect until new or revised contracts are agreed to; and nothing in the bill is to deprive any employee of a transportation entity of their rights, privileges, obligations, or status regarding any pension or retirement system or to affect the civil service status, if any, of the employees.  Any rules and regulations adopted by the transportation entities are to continue in effect as rules and regulations adopted by the department until the rules and regulations are amended, supplemented, or rescinded by the department.  The department is permitted to adopt rules and regulations modifying or repealing the rules and regulations of a transportation entity pursuant to a procedure prescribed in the bill.  After the Transfer Date, neither the department nor any transportation entity is permitted to issue new bonds.  Moreover, the bill prohibits the department from using any asset transferred to the department by a transportation entity for non-transportation related purposes.

     As soon as practicable after the Transfer Date, the department is to notify the Governor and the presiding officers of each house of the Legislature that the transfer has occurred, of the date of the transfer, and of any other information concerning the transfer as the department deems appropriate.

     The New Jersey transportation system is of vital importance to its residents and the nation.  Due to its size and significance, it requires a greater level of government oversight to ensure that all persons and industries served by the States transportation system receive high quality and safe transportation service.  With this legislation, the sponsor seeks to streamline operations, reduce bureaucracy, and improve accountability within the States transportation sector and thereby mend the fragmented nature of the States transportation system, which has resulted in inefficiencies, rising costs, and reliability issues.
