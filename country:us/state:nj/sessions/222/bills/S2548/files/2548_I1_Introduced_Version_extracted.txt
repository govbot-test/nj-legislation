STATEMENT This bill would require additional information regarding the assessment of real property to be provided in the assessment notifications required to be mailed to property taxpayers annually. Current law requires each municipal assessor to annually provide each of New Jersey혪s property owners with access to, and notice of, their annual property assessment.혻 Information about a property owner혪s assessment is important, because the assessment is the basis of the calculation of the annual property tax bill.혻 Current law requires public advertisement of the ability to review the municipality혪s tax lists and individual mailing of the assessment notice to each taxpayer.혻 The existing notice provided to taxpayers lists only the prior year혪s taxes and the current year혪s assessed value.혻 The purpose of this bill is to provide property owners with a significantly better understanding as to how they are being taxed by expanding the contents of the assessment notice that is currently required by current law. The bill would require the addition of the following information in the assessment notice: With respect to the prior tax year:혻 the prior year's taxes; the prior year혪s final assessment; the Director혪s ratio of assessed to true value in the taxing district or an assessed to market value ratio of 100 percent if a revaluation or reassessment was implemented; and the implied prior year혪s market value calculated by dividing the prior year혪s final assessment by either the Director혪s ratio, or by 100 percent if a revaluation or reassessment was implemented. With respect to the current tax year: the current year혪s assessment; the Director혪s ratio of assessed to true value in the taxing district or an assessed to market value ratio of 100 percent if a revaluation or reassessment was implemented; and the estimated market value calculated by dividing the current year혪s assessment by either the Director혪s ratio, or by 100 percent if a revaluation or reassessment was implemented.
