A1103 ASSEMBLY, No. 1103 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 222nd LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2026 SESSION Sponsored by: Assemblyman JOE DANIELSEN District 17 (Middlesex and Somerset) Assemblywoman TENNILLE R. MCCOY District 14 (Mercer and Middlesex) Co-Sponsored by: Assemblywoman Murphy SYNOPSIS Exempts experienced volunteer firefighters from certain requirements. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. An Act concerning firefighters and supplementing Title 40A of the New Jersey Statutes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.   A fire department or force, whether part-paid or volunteer, shall not prohibit a volunteer firefighter who has five or more years of experience in fire suppression, firefighting, or fire rescue from engaging in fire suppression, firefighting, or fire rescue solely on the grounds that the volunteer firefighter is uncertified or does not meet a requirement that is not imposed pursuant to statute, rule, or regulation. 2.    This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT Under the bill, part-paid and volunteer fire departments would not be permitted to prohibit a volunteer firefighter who has five or more years of experience in fire suppression, firefighting, or fire rescue from engaging in these activities solely on the basis that the volunteer firefighter is uncertified or does not meet any requirement that is not imposed pursuant to statute, rule, or regulation.
