Section 1:
S380 SENATE, No. 380 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 222nd LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2026 SESSION Sponsored by: Senator DOUGLAS J. STEINHARDT District 23 (Hunterdon, Somerset and Warren) SYNOPSIS Requires public utilities to assume liability for certain work performed by third-party contractors. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. An Act requiring public utilities to assume liability for certain work performed by third-party contractors and supplementing Title 48 of the Revised Statutes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.    As used in P.L.    , c. (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill): Complaint means a concern, difficulty, or problem that a customer brings to the attention of a public utility, verbally or in writing, without the involvement of the New Jersey Board of Public Utilities. Customer means a residential customer or commercial customer of the public utility. Public utility means a public utility, as defined pursuant to R.S.48:2-13, that provides electric, gas, sewer, or water service to end users in the State. Third-party contractor means an individual or company contracted by a public utility to install or upgrade equipment used to provide electric, gas, sewer, or water service to customers. 2.    a.  A public utility shall be responsible for responding to and resolving a customers complaint arising from non-elective work performed or services rendered by a third-party contractor. b.    Each public utility shall establish and implement an internal process for documenting the public utilitys response to and resolution of a customers complaint.  At a minimum, the public utility shall document: (1)  the name and address of the customer who initiated the complaint and the date the customer filed the complaint; (2)  a description of the facts underlying the customers complaint, including the date of the alleged incident giving rise to the customers complaint; (3)  all communications between the public utility and the customer concerning the complaint; and (4)  the public utilitys final resolution of the customers complaint. 3.    a.  A public utility shall assume liability for any property damage caused by or arising from work performed or services rendered by a third-party contractor, unless a contract executed between the public utility and third-party contractor prior to the effective date of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) expressly provides otherwise. b.    Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit a public utility from entering into an agreement with a third-party contractor regarding indemnification or contribution. 4.    Nothing in P.L.    , c. (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) shall be construed to apply to complaints arising from elective work or services performed by a third-party contractor, which work or services were requested by a customer. 5.    This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT Under this bill, a public utility is responsible for responding to and resolving a customers complaint arising from non-elective work performed or services rendered by a third-party contractor.  The bill requires each public utility to establish and implement an internal process for documenting the public utilitys resolution of a customers complaint, as outlined in the bill. Additionally, the bill requires a public utility to assume liability for any property damage caused by or arising from work performed or services rendered by a third-party contractor, unless a contract executed between the public utility and third-party contractor prior to the effective date of the bill expressly provides otherwise.  The bill provides that the provisions of the bill are not to be construed to prohibit a public utility from entering into an agreement with a third-party contractor regarding indemnification or contribution. Finally, the bill clarifies that the provisions of the bill do not apply to elective work performed or services rendered by a third-party contractor at the request of a customer. Under the bill, complaint is defined as a concern, difficulty, or problem that a customer brings to the attention of a public utility, verbally or in writing, without the involvement of the New Jersey Board of Public Utilities.  Additionally, the bill defines third-party contractor as an individual or company contracted by a public utility to install or upgrade equipment used to provide electric, gas, sewer, or water service to customers.
