S2140 SENATE, No. 2140 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 222nd LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2026 SESSION Sponsored by: Senator PAUL D. MORIARTY District 4 (Atlantic, Camden and Gloucester) SYNOPSIS Prohibits providers of commercial mobile service and developers of mobile application from disclosing customer's global position system data to third parties under certain circumstances. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. An Act concerning commercial mobile service providers and global positioning system data and supplementing Title 56 of the Revised Statutes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.В В В  As used in P.L.В В В  , c.В В В  (C.В В В В В  ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill): В“Commercial mobile serviceВ” means a type of mobile telecommunications service as defined in subsection (d) of section 332 of the Communications Act of 1934 (47 U.S.C. s.332(d)). В“Commercial mobile service providerВ” or В“providerВ” means an individual, proprietorship, partnership, corporation, association, or other legal entity that provides commercial mobile service on a mobile device. В“CustomerВ” means an individual within this State who provides, either knowingly or unknowingly, GPS data to a commercial mobile service provider or a mobile device application developer in the course of using the providerВ’s service or the developerВ’s application on a mobile device. В“DiscloseВ” means to release, transfer, share, disseminate, make available, sell, or otherwise communicate by any means to a third party a customerВ’s GPS data. В“Global positioning system dataВ” or В“GPS dataВ” means a customerВ’s physical location information collected by a global positioning system on a mobile device that is accessible to a commercial mobile service provider or a mobile device application developer. В“Mobile deviceВ” means wireless telecommunications device that is capable of collecting a customerВ’s GPS data. В“Mobile device applicationВ” means an application software designed to run on a mobile device, such as a smartphone or tablet computer. В“Mobile device application developerВ” or В“developerВ” means any person, including corporate affiliates, that stores or processes a customerВ’s GPS data collected by a mobile device application. В“PersonВ” means a natural person, corporation, association, partnership, or other legal entity. В“Third partyВ” means an individual, proprietorship, partnership, corporation, association, or other legal entity that may knowingly or willfully disclose a customerВ’s GPS data. 2.В В В  a.В  A commercial mobile service provider that provides commercial mobile service to a customer or a developer of mobile device applications shall not disclose the customerВ’s global positioning system data to a third party, unless the customer has given consent for the third party to access the customerВ’s GPS data. In order to obtain consent from a customer for a third party to access the customerВ’s GPS data, a mobile device application developer shall provide the following notice in bold typeface to which the customer shall affirmatively elect: В“I agree to allow my location data to be disclosed to a third party.В” b.В В В  A third party that accesses a customerВ’s GPS data pursuant to subsection a. of this section shall not sell the GPS data in any case, and shall disclose the GPS data otherwise only as necessary to effectuate the purpose for which consent was given. c.В В В В  The provisions of subsection a. of this section shall not apply to a commercial mobile service provider, developer of a mobile device application, or a third party required to disclose a customerВ’s GPS data to comply with applicable federal or State law, regulation, law enforcement investigation, legal process, or court order. 3.В В В  It shall be an unlawful practice and violation of P.L.1960, c.39 (C.56:8-1 et seq.) for a commercial mobile service provider, mobile device application developer, or a third party to disclose a customerВ’s GPS data in violation of section 2 of P.L.В В В В ,В c.В В В В (C.В В В В В В )В (pending before the Legislature as this bill). 4.В В В  The Director of the Division of Consumer Affairs in the Department of Law and Public Safety shall promulgate rules and regulations, pursuant to the В“Administrative Procedure Act,В” P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et seq.), necessary to effectuate the purposes of P.L.В В В  , c.В В В  (C.В В В В В  ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill). 5.В В В  This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This bill prohibits commercial mobile service providers and mobile device application developers from disclosing a customerВ’s global positioning system (GPS) data to a third party, unless the customer has given consent for the third party to access the customerВ’s GPS data.В  In order to obtain a customerВ’s consent, the bill requires a mobile device application developer to provide notice to the customer, which customer would be required to affirmatively elect to allow their location data to be disclosed to a third party. However, the bill provides that these entities would not be required to obtain a customerВ’s consent before disclosing any GPS data that is required to comply with a law or regulation, law enforcement investigation, legal process, or court order. Under the bill, a third party that accesses a customerВ’s GPS data would also be prohibited from selling the customerВ’s data in any circumstances. Additionally, the bill provides that the third party may only disclose the customerВ’s GPS data as necessary to effectuate the purpose for which the consent was given. A commercial mobile service provider, mobile device application developer, or third party that violates the provisions of this bill would be deemed to violate the StateВ’s consumer fraud act, which may result in a penalty of not more than $10,000 for the first offense and not more than $20,000 for each subsequent offense.
