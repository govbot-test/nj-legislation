An Act concerning neonatal care and amending P.L.1992, c.160.

 

     Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

 

     1. Section 19 of P.L.1992, c.160 (C.26:2H-7a) is amended to read as follows:

     19.  Notwithstanding the provisions of section 7 of P.L.1971, c.136 (C.26:2H-7) to the contrary, the following are exempt from the certificate of need requirement:

     Community-based primary care centers;

     Outpatient drug and alcohol services;

     Hospital-based medical detoxification for drugs and alcohol;

     Ambulance and invalid coach services;

     Mental health services which are non-bed related outpatient services;

     Full service diagnostic catheterization services, primary angioplasty services, and elective angioplasty services in accordance with a license issued under section 2 of P.L.2021, c.18 (C.26:2H-7.23);

     Residential health care facility services;

     Dementia care homes;

     Capital improvements and renovations to health care facilities;

     Additions of medical/surgical, adult intensive care and adult critical care beds in hospitals;

     A 25 percent increase in the number of intermediate or intensive bassinets in a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, irrespective of the changes that the Department of Health shall make to the certificate of need process for neonatal care unit expansions, as required pursuant to the provisions of this act; however, a reduction of intermediate or intensive bassinets in a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, shall not be exempt from the certificate of need requirement;

     An increase in the level of neonatal care designation of a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, with the exception of a health care facility with a designation as a regional perinatal center, shall be exempt from the certificate of need requirement; however, a health care facility with a designation as a regional perinatal center or a decrease in the level of neonatal care designation of a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, shall not be exempt from the certificate of need requirement;

     Within 180 days following the effective date of this act, the Department of Health shall revise the certificate of need process for neonatal care unit expansions in a manner that reflects the current needs and expectations of parents in this State, with special consideration for reducing the need to transfer neonatal care unit patients between hospitals;

     Inpatient special psychiatric beds used solely for services for patients with co-occurring mental health and substance use disorders;

     Replacement of existing major moveable equipment;

     Inpatient operating rooms;

     Alternate family care programs;

     Hospital-based subacute care;

     Ambulatory care facilities;

     Comprehensive outpatient rehabilitation services;

     Special child health clinics;

     New technology in accordance with the provisions of section 18 of P.L.1998, c.43 (C.26:2H-7d);

     Transfer of ownership interest except in the case of an acute care hospital;

     Change of site for approved certificate of need within the same county;

     Additions to vehicles or hours of operation of a mobile intensive care unit;

     Relocation or replacement of a health care facility within the same county, except for an acute care hospital;

     Continuing care retirement communities authorized pursuant to P.L.1986, c.103 (C.52:27D-330 et seq.);

     Magnetic resonance imaging;

     Adult day health care facilities;

     Pediatric day health care facilities;

     Chronic or acute renal dialysis facilities; and

     Transfer of ownership of a hospital to an authority in accordance with P.L.2006, c.46 (C.30:9-23.15 et al.).

(cf: P.L.2021, c.18, s.4)

 

     2.  This act shall take effect immediately.

 

 

STATEMENT

 

      This bill expands New Jerseys certificate of need exemption list. 

     Under New Jerseys certificate of need requirement, an application for and receipt of a certificate of need from the Department of Health is required for the construction of a health care facility, the expansion of a health care facility, or the institution of a new health care service.

     This bill provides exemptions from New Jerseys certificate of need requirement for: 1) a 25 percent increase in the number of intermediate or intensive bassinets in a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, irrespective of the changes that the Department of Health is to make to the certificate of need process for neonatal care unit expansions; however, a reduction of intermediate or intensive bassinets in a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, is not to be exempt from the certificate of need requirement; and 2) an increase in the level of neonatal care designation of a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, with the exception of a health care facility with a designation as a regional perinatal center, is to be exempt from the certificate of need requirement, however, a health care facility with a designation as a reginal perinatal center or a decrease in the level of neonatal care designation of a health care facility that provides inpatient, maternity services, is not to be exempt from the certificate of need requirement;

     Within 180 days following the bills effective date, the Department of Health is to revise the certificate of need process for neonatal care unit expansions in a manner that reflects the current needs and expectations of parents in this State, with special consideration for reducing the need to transfer neonatal care unit patients between hospitals.
