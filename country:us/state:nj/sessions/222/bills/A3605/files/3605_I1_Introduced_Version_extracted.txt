A3605 ASSEMBLY, No. 3605 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 222nd LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2026 SESSION Sponsored by: Assemblyman LOUIS D. GREENWALD District 6 (Burlington and Camden) SYNOPSIS Supplemental appropriation of $1 million from General Fund to DHS for Larc Norcross School Special Needs Adult Program transportation. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. A Supplement to the annual appropriations act for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2026, P.L.2025, c.74. Be It Enacted by the Senate and the General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.  In addition to the amounts appropriated under P.L.2025, c.74, the annual appropriations act for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2026, there is appropriated from the General Fund the following amount for the purpose specified: 54 DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES 30 Educational, Cultural, and Intellectual Development 32 Operation and Support of Educational Institutions 7601 Community Programs GRANTS-IN-AID 03-7601  Adult Activities .................................................... $1,000,000 Total Grant-in-Aid appropriation, Community Programs ...................................... $1,000,000 Grant-in Aid: 03    Larc Norcross School Special Needs Adult Program Transportation ............................................................. ($1,000,000) 2.    This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This bill makes an FY2026 supplemental appropriation of $1 million to the Division of Developmental Disabilities in the Department of Human Services for the Larc Norcross School Special Needs Adult Program transportation. The Larc Norcross School Special Needs Adult Program serves individuals with disabilities over the age of 21, filling a critical need for this population as it transitions out of educational programming.  Through the provision of daily life care supports, therapy consultation, and recreational and socialization opportunities, the program provides participants with a respectful environment of continued growth and independence.  Transportation is key to participation in this day program.  While most participants receive Medicaid coverage, transportation funding under Medicaid is inadequate.  These additional State funds will ensure participants have consistent and reliable access to transportation in order to receive the beneficial services provided by the Larc Norcross School Special Needs Adult Program.
