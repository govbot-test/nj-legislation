ACR71 ASSEMBLY CONCURRENT RESOLUTION No. 71 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 222nd LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2026 SESSION Sponsored by: Assemblywoman ANNETTE QUIJANO District 20 (Union) Co-Sponsored by: Assemblyman Danielsen SYNOPSIS Urges U.S. President and Congress to enact "Visible Identification Standards for Immigration-Based Law Enforcement Act of 2025." CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. A Concurrent Resolution urging the President and the Congress of the United States to enact the Visible Identification Standards for ImmigrationBased Law Enforcement Act of 2025. Whereas, Transparency and accountability are essential to maintaining public trust and upholding constitutional governance; and Whereas, The State of New Jersey is committed to protecting the rights and dignity of all individuals and ensuring that enforcement actions are conducted with respect and fairness; and Whereas, The display of visible identification by immigration enforcement officers is essential to ensure that individuals are aware of the identity and authority of the officers conducting enforcement actions; and Whereas, Because impersonations of federal agents are increasing, the requirement for visible identification will help prevent potential abuses of power and misconduct by providing a clear mechanism for accountability; and Whereas, The Visible Identification Standards for ImmigrationBased Law Enforcement Act of 2025, also known as the VISIBLE Act, was introduced on July 8, 2025 in the United States Senate as S.2212 and aims to enhance transparency and accountability in immigration enforcement by requiring immigration enforcement officers to display visible identification during public-facing actions; and Whereas, Many immigration enforcement activities have recently taken place in New Jersey, and more are expected to occur; and Whereas, The VISIBLE Act aligns with the values of transparency, accountability, and justice that are fundamental to the principles of the State of New Jersey; now, therefore, Be It Resolved by the General Assembly of the State of New Jersey (the Senate concurring): 1.    The Legislature of this State respectfully urges the President and the Congress and of the United States to enact the Visible Identification Standards for Immigration-Based Law Enforcement Act of 2025 to ensure the authenticity and accountability of officers designated to conduct immigration enforcement. 2.    Copies of this resolution, as filed with the Secretary of the State, shall be transmitted by the Clerk of the General Assembly or the Secretary of the Senate to the President and Vice President of the United States, the Majority and Minority Leaders of the United States Senate, the Speaker and Minority Leader of the United States House of Representatives, and every member of Congress elected from this State, and the Secretary of the Department of Homeland Security. STATEMENT This resolution respectfully urges Congress and the President to enact the VISIBLE Act introduced in the United States Senate as S.2212 on July 8, 2025. The VISIBLE Act, also known as the Visible Identification Standards for Immigration-Based Law Enforcement Act of 2025, requires officers of the U.S. Customs and Border Protection, U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement, or any individual authorized, deputized, or designated to perform immigration enforcement to wear visible identification during the performance of public immigration enforcement functions. Under the VISIBLE Act, immigration officers would be required to display the full name or widely recognized initials of the officers employing agency and the officers last name or badge number.  Non-medical face coverings are also prohibited under the VISIBLE Act unless operationally necessary for covert operations or environmental hazards. The VISIBLE Act requires the Secretary of Homeland Security to discipline officers who fail to comply with the bills provisions and additionally requires the secretary to issue an annual report to the Office for Civil Rights and Civil Liberties within the Department of Homeland Security and to several congressional committees regarding the enforcement of the bills provisions.  The VISIBLE Act further requires the Office for Civil Liberties to investigate complaints by the public on certain immigration enforcement actions, issue recommendations to relevant department components regarding compliance and corrective action, and include this information in its annual public report. Requiring officers engaged in public immigration actions to clearly identify themselves is crucial for the safety of the public.  Authorities in several states have arrested impersonators of Immigration and Customs Enforcement officers.  As immigration enforcement actions are expected to surge, it becomes increasingly important to ensure the authenticity and accountability of the authorities designated to do this work.
